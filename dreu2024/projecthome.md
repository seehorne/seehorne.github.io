## About My Mentors

### PI
My PI is Professor [Anhong Guo](https://guoanhong.com/) from the University of Michigan Ann Arbor. Anhong is an Assistant Professor of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at the University of Michigan in the CSE (Computer Science and Engineering) department, and he also has a courtesy appointment as Assistant Professor with the School of Information. At University of Michigan, Anhong directs the Human-AI Lab (otherwise known as HAIL), which is part of the broader [Human-Centered Computing Lab](https://hcc.engin.umich.edu/)—home to groups in the Michigan CSE Division who investigate Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Educational Technology, Multimedia, and Social Computing. Anhong's research, which sits at the intersection of HCI and Artificial Intelligence, is orientied towards designing, developing, studying, and deploying human-AI intelligent interactive systems to enhance the accessibility of the real world and the digital world alike. 


### PhD Student
My PhD student mentor is [Jaylin Herskovitz](https://jayl.in/): a 5th year PhD student in the University of Michigan CSE department advised by Anhong Guo. Her research focuses on creating augmented reality and AI-based tools for accessibility and collaboration, with the goal of helping people create customized AI and sensing technologies to suit their unique needs. Her most recent work—including the project I am working with her on this summer—centers on the theme of supporting people with disabilities (particularly Blind/Low Vision (BLV) folks) in maximizing the utility of AI-based assistive technologies. She approaches this theme in domains ranging from understanding the behaviors of hacking, switching, and combining utilized by BLV people in making imperfect interfaces usable, to tool development and end-user programming techniques to enable creation of customized DIY tools, to augmenting of existing tools for better intention-matching and explainability.
